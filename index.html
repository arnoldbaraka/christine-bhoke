About Mama Arno

Christine Bhoke Nchamah, fondly known as Mama Arno, is a proud daughter of the Kuria West community, a mother of three, and a national leader in community empowerment. She balances family, leadership, and politics with grace and wisdom. Her firstborn, Arnold (“Arno”), serves the nation as a top DevSecOps engineer at Safaricom, deploying critical digital health systems — proof that leadership begins at home and blossoms into service for the country.

Public Service Timeline

  • NG-CDF Kuria West: Advocated for fair resource allocation and transparency.
  • Lake Basin Authority Collaborations: Supported projects linking infrastructure and community uplift.
  • Board Member, Communications Authority of Kenya: Contributed to national ICT & communications policy.
  • Women’s Forums: Speaker, mentor, and mobilizer across counties.

Vision & Mission

  • Empower women & youth to rise into leadership positions.
  • Advance education and healthcare in Kuria West and beyond.
  • Bridge the digital divide for marginalized communities.
  • Promote inclusive governance across Kenya.

Funding & Support Opportunities

  • Gold Tier (KES 5M+): Schools for girls, maternal health centers, leadership institutes.
  • Green Tier (KES 1M–5M): Digital hubs, small infrastructure (wells, classrooms), youth mentorship.
  • Community Tier (KES 100k–1M): Women’s cooperatives, small business support, farm empowerment.

Every shilling invested in Christine’s initiatives builds futures for women and communities at the margins.

Strengths

  • Deep-rooted connection to Kuria culture and people.
  • Proven leadership in national and regional bodies.
  • Dedication to women’s empowerment.
  • Bridge between tradition and digital transformation.
